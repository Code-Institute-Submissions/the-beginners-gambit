Setting up the chess board

  1. Orient the board

    Important!
    Both players should have a light square on the bottom right corner facing toward them.

    Why?
    This makes it so the queen is placed on her own colored square.

    The chess board is also divided into ranks and files. Ranks represent the vertical rows of the board 1➔8 whereas files represent the columns a➔h.

  2. Place the pawns

    The white pawns are placed across the 2nd rank and black pawns across the 7th rank.

  3. Place the rooks

    Rooks go on the corners of the board on their respective sides.

  4. Place the knights and bishops

    The knight and bishop are what’s known as “minor pieces”. The knights go next to the rooks and the bishops the square after.

  5. place the king and queen

    The queen is always placed on a square of her own color. The king slots into the last remaining square.
